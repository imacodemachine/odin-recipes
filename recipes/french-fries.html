French Fries Recipe

Description

French fries are crispy, golden potato strips that are beloved worldwide. The secret to perfect fries is double-frying: a low-temperature fry to cook the interior, followed by a high-temperature fry for that irresistible crispy exterior. Serve them hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

Ingredients

  • Russet Potatoes
  • Vegetable Oil (for frying)
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Peel the potatoes (optional) and cut them into evenly-sized sticks, about ¼ to ½ inch thick.
  2. Rinse the cut potatoes in cold water to remove excess starch. Soak them in cold water for at least 30 minutes (or up to a few hours) for crispier results.
  3. Drain the potatoes and pat them completely dry with paper towels or a clean kitchen towel.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pot or fryer to 325°F (160°C). Carefully add potatoes in batches—don't overcrowd the pot.
  5. Fry for 5-7 minutes until the potatoes are cooked through but still pale. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  6. Increase the oil temperature to 375°F (190°C). Return the fries to the oil in batches.
  7. Fry for 2-4 minutes until golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain on fresh paper towels.
  8. Season immediately with salt while still hot. Serve with ketchup, aioli, or your favorite dipping sauce.
