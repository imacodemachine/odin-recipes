Mapo Tofu Receipe

Description

Mapo tofu is a classic Sichuan dish of soft tofu simmered in a spicy, aromatic sauce made with fermented bean paste, chili oil, garlic, and minced meat (usually pork or beef). It's known for its bold málà flavor—spicy heat balanced by the tongue-tingling numbness of Sichuan peppercorns.

Ingredients

  • Soft Tofu
  • Ground Pork
  • Doubanjiang
  • Garlic
  • Chili Oil
  • Sichuan Peppercorns
  • Soy Sauce
  • Chicken Stock
  • Vegetable Oil
  • Cornstarch

Steps

  1. Gently cut tofu into cubes. Optionally warm it in hot (not boiling) water for a minute, then drain—this helps it stay intact.
  2. Heat neutral oil in a pan over medium heat. Add ground pork (or beef) and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Add minced garlic and ginger. Stir briefly until fragrant, then add doubanjiang and a little chili oil. Cook until the oil turns red and aromatic.
  4. Pour in chicken stock (or water) and a splash of soy sauce. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  5. Carefully slide in the tofu cubes. Gently stir or shake the pan so the tofu doesn’t break. Simmer for a few minutes to absorb flavor.
  6. Mix cornstarch with a little water, then slowly stir it into the pan until the sauce thickens.
  7. Sprinkle in Sichuan peppercorns (to taste) and drizzle a bit more chili oil. Give it one last gentle stir.
  8. Serve hot with rice.
